M&A Integration Decision Framework

A structured framework used to determine whether a target company should be fully integrated, partially segmented, ring-fenced or rebuilt in a clean-room environment during M&A.

1. Core Integration Patterns

  • Full Integration – Target meets minimum cyber hygiene; low risk.
  • Segmented Integration – Some risks exist; isolate high-risk assets.
  • Ring-Fenced – High-risk environment must remain isolated.
  • Clean-Room Rebuild – Critical compromise; rebuild from zero.

2. Decision Criteria

Factor Low-Risk Medium High / Critical
Operating Systems Fully supported Some outdated systems Multiple legacy critical servers
Breach History No breaches Minor isolated incident Major breach / active compromise
Identity & Access MFA everywhere MFA partial No MFA / shared admin accounts
Cloud/SaaS Secure, compliant Some misconfigurations Unsecured cloud, public exposure

3. Integration Decision Matrix

Risk Level Recommended Action Why Typical Timeline
Low Risk Full Integration Meets hygiene, no major findings 2–4 weeks
Medium Risk Segmented Integration Issues exist but can be contained 4–8 weeks
High Risk Ring-Fence Critical vulnerabilities or major gaps 8–12 weeks
Critical Clean-Room Rebuild Active breach, ransomware, deep compromise 12–20 weeks
