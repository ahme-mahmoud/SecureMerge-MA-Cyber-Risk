SecureMerge – Executive Summary

SecureMerge is an M&A-focused Cyber Risk Assessment & Governance Framework designed to support organizations during mergers and acquisitions by identifying inherited cyber risks, scoring their severity, and recommending evidence-based integration strategies.

Project Purpose

Parent companies acquiring smaller SaaS providers or service firms often discover hidden risks: outdated systems, weak IAM, shadow IT, and unknown prior breaches. SecureMerge reduces this uncertainty by providing:

  • A structured Due Diligence Questionnaire
  • A scoring engine to classify cyber maturity
  • Integration & isolation decision recommendations
  • Governance models (RACI + remediation timelines)

M&A Due Diligence Process (Diagram)

1. Collect Evidence →
2. Assess Controls →
3. Score Risks →
4. Classify (Low / Medium / High) →
5. Recommend Strategy →
6. Integrate / Isolate / Ring-Fence

Integration Strategy Options

  • Full Integration – for low-risk targets
  • Segmented Integration – medium risk with partial issues
  • Ring-Fenced – major issues or breaches
  • Clean-Room Rebuild – for severely compromised environments

Risk Appetite Table

Risk Level Allowed During Acquisition? Max Tolerated Time Notes
Low Risk Yes 90 days Standard integration
Medium Risk Conditionally 45 days Requires mitigation plan
High Risk No 0 days Isolate or rebuild

Conclusion

SecureMerge provides a complete M&A cyber risk governance framework enabling business leaders to take informed decisions regarding integration, isolation, or divestment. The platform supports scalability, repeatability, and consistent cyber due diligence.
