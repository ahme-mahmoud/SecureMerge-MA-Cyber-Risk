M&A Integration Decision Framework

This framework provides guidance for deciding how to integrate a target company based on its cyber maturity and overall risk score.

1. Integration Patterns

  • Full Integration – Directly merge systems & identities, normal controls.
  • Segmented Integration – Low-risk assets merge, high-risk isolated temporarily.
  • Ring-Fenced – All systems remain isolated behind a strong boundary.
  • Clean-Room Rebuild – Critical rebuild before connecting anything.

2. Decision Criteria

  • Overall risk score (from the assessment).
  • Existence of breaches in the last 12 months.
  • Maturity of identity & logging controls.
  • Exposure to third-party / SaaS risks.
  • Cloud configuration and backups posture.
  • Criticality of inherited systems.

3. Integration Scenarios

1) Low-Risk SaaS Startup Low Risk

Recommended Decision: Full Integration

A young SaaS startup with modern infrastructure, MFA everywhere, cloud-native security controls, and no known breaches. Minimal legacy systems.

  • Why: High cyber hygiene, compliant SaaS stack, strong IAM.
  • Timeline: 2–3 weeks for full directory & network merging.

2) Medium-Risk Regional Service Provider Medium Risk

Recommended Decision: Segmented Integration

Uses a mix of modern systems and some outdated on-prem servers. Logging partial. No major breaches but visibility is limited.

  • Why: Some controls missing, minor vulnerabilities.
  • Timeline: 4–6 weeks until remediation of outdated systems.

3) High-Risk Manufacturing Company High Risk

Recommended Decision: Ring-Fenced Environment

Contains legacy Windows servers, no MFA, missing logs, weak segmentation, and previous malware incidents.

  • Why: High threat exposure + high impact systems.
  • Timeline: 2–3 months of remediation before connecting.

4) Critical Breach Detected Pre-Acquisition Critical

Recommended Decision: Clean-Room Rebuild

Incident response shows active compromise; identity systems and servers may be backdoored.

  • Why: Cannot trust any inherited system.
  • Timeline: 3–6 months of full rebuild before any integration.
