Integration Scenarios

Realistic examples of how different acquired companies are integrated based on their cyber risk posture.

Scenario 1: Low-Risk SaaS Startup – Full Integration
Risk Level Low (22%)
Decision Full Integration into parent company’s network & identity systems.
Why - Modern cloud architecture
- Strong IAM with enforced MFA
- Good logging & vulnerability management
- No known breaches or critical gaps
Timeline 2–4 weeks (standard onboarding & API integration)
Scenario 2: Medium-Risk Regional Provider – Segmented Integration
Risk Level Medium (48%)
Decision Segmented Integration – high-risk systems temporarily isolated.
Why - Mixed environment (cloud + on-prem)
- MFA partially deployed
- Limited SaaS logging
- Some outdated OS in financial department
Timeline 6–10 weeks (remediation → partial integration → full merge)
Scenario 3: High-Risk Manufacturing Company – Ring-Fenced Environment
Risk Level High (68%)
Decision Ring-Fenced – no connectivity until critical issues are resolved.
Why - Multiple unsupported Windows servers
- No SIEM or monitoring
- Indicators of past unreported breaches
- Shared privileged accounts
Timeline 3–6 months (patching → IAM overhaul → logging → reassessment)
Scenario 4: Critical Breach – Clean-Room Rebuild
Risk Level Critical (88%)
Decision Clean-Room Rebuild before any connection to parent network.
Why - Active compromise detected
- Attack persistence suspected
- No viable logging to verify system integrity
- Unsafe to integrate or reuse existing images
Timeline 4–8 months (forensic investigation → rebuild → controlled onboarding)
